Kibo is a digital kakeibo — a Japanese practice of mindful spending. Not a tracker. Not a budget enforcer. A monthly act of honest reflection.

The Philosophy

A 120-year-old practice, still ahead of its time

Kakeibo (家計簿) was invented in 1904 by Hani Motoko, Japan's first female journalist. She believed that most people overspend not from lack of self-control, but from lack of awareness. The practice she designed asks you to record every expense by hand — because the act of writing is itself the intervention.

Most budgeting apps automate tracking in order to remove effort. Kibo asks for the opposite. The deliberate pause of logging an expense by hand is where the value lives.

Every month, four questions

The structure Kakeibo was built around

Traditional kakeibo begins each month not with a budget spreadsheet, but with four honest questions — asked in order. Kibo guides you through each one.

一
How much do you have available?

Start with what's real. Subtract your fixed obligations — rent, bills, subscriptions — from your income. This is your true available amount, not what you earn but what you actually control.

二
How much would you like to save?

This is the critical difference. Savings is not what's left over. It is a commitment made before the first purchase — an untouchable amount set aside for your future self, first. You live on the remainder.

三
How much are you spending?

Log each expense into one of four pillars. The friction of writing it down is the point — it turns a passive transaction into a conscious choice, and reveals patterns that automation quietly hides.

四
How can you improve?

At the end of the month, you reflect — not to judge yourself, but to understand yourself. What did your spending say about what you value? What would you choose differently?

The Four Pillars

Every expense has a home

Kakeibo doesn't sort spending by merchant or category tag. It sorts by purpose — by the role each expense plays in your life. This is what makes the monthly reflection meaningful rather than mathematical.

Needs · 必要
Survival

Groceries, rent, transport, utilities, medicine. The essentials your life depends on.

Wants · 欲望
Optional

Dining out, new clothes, hobbies. Not bad — but consciously chosen, not automatically justified.

Culture · 文化
Growth

Books, courses, museums, concerts. Spending that invests in who you are becoming.

Unexpected · 特別
Extra

Repairs, gifts, emergencies. Life doesn't follow a plan. This pillar holds what didn't.

Mindful Friction

A digital app that honours the physical practice

Traditional kakeibo is done with pen and paper. The physical act of writing creates a moment of friction that forces you to process a purchase — to own it, consciously. Kibo reintroduces this friction deliberately.

On Logging
Pause before recording

When logging a Want, Kibo asks why. Not to judge — to make the choice visible to yourself.

On Saving
Save first

Your savings goal is set at the start of the month and treated as fixed. You plan around it, not after it.

On Categories
Pillar envelopes

Allocate your spending budget across the four pillars at the month's start. Track against each one independently.

On Reflection
Monthly review

A dedicated space at month's end to review your Needs-to-Wants ratio, celebrate wins, and write your next month's intention.

Privacy by Design

Your financial life belongs to you

Kibo is a local-first application. Everything you record — every budget, every expense, every reflection — lives encrypted on your device. There are no servers, no accounts, no way for us to see what you write. What you spend and how you reflect is entirely yours.

Kibo isn't about being perfect. It's about being present.

Privacy Policy

Your financial life is private. Kibo is designed that way from the ground up — not as a policy, but as an architecture.

Local-first, always

All data — your budgets, expenses, pillar allocations, and monthly reflections — is stored exclusively on your device using encrypted local storage. We have no servers that hold your data, no accounts that identify you, and no technical means of accessing what you record.

No tracking, no analytics

Kibo does not collect usage data, telemetry, crash reports linked to your identity, or any behavioral analytics. We do not know you are using the app. There is no way for us to know.

No advertising

Kibo does not serve ads and does not share data with advertising networks. There is no data to share.

Your responsibility

Because your data never leaves your device, you are responsible for keeping it backed up. If you lose your device without a backup, your records cannot be recovered by us or by anyone else. Please back up your device regularly.
